The 5 Prompt Rules
That Change Everything

Stop prompting like a beginner. Start getting 10x better results from Claude.

Most people type one sentence into Claude and wonder why the output feels generic. These five rules will transform how you prompt and the results you get.

1
Step One

Context First, Task Second

✗
Before
"Write me an email to my supplier"
↓
✓
After
"I'm an Amazon seller. My supplier shipped the wrong quantity last week. Write a firm but professional email requesting a correction and credit."
💡
Why It Works

Claude can't read your mind. The more context you give upfront, the less back and forth you need.

2
Step Two

Show, Don't Tell

✗
Before
"Write in a casual tone"
↓
✓
After
"Here's how I write emails: [paste an actual email]. Write in this same style."
💡
Why It Works

One real example beats a paragraph of descriptions. Claude learns patterns from examples better than from adjectives.

3
Step Three

Break Big Tasks into Steps

✗
Before
"Create my entire marketing plan"
↓
✓
After
"Step 1: Define my target audience for [product]. Step 2: Write 3 positioning messages for that audience. Step 3: Suggest the top 3 channels to reach them."
💡
Why It Works

Smaller tasks mean more focused answers. You also get to course correct between steps.

4
Step Four

Tell Claude What NOT to Do

✗
Before
"Write a product description"
↓
✓
After
"Write a product description for [product]. Don't use buzzwords like 'revolutionary' or 'game changing.' Don't exceed 150 words. Don't include a call to action."
💡
Why It Works

Constraints sharpen output. Telling Claude what to avoid is often more useful than telling it what to include.

5
Step Five

Iterate, Don't Restart

✗
Before
Starting a new conversation when Claude's output isn't perfect
↓
✓
After
"Good start, but make the tone more direct. Remove the second paragraph. Add a specific number for the discount."
💡
Why It Works

Claude learns from feedback within the same conversation. Each follow up message improves the output. Starting over means losing all that context.

⚡
Quick Reference
1
Context First Give background before the task
2
Show, Don't Tell Paste a real example, not adjectives
3
Break It Down Split big requests into numbered steps
4
Say What NOT to Do Constraints produce sharper results
5
Iterate, Don't Restart Refine in the same conversation
