Act as an expert Casting Director and AI Character Designer.

I will paste a Brand File below. Your job is to create one single Character Blueprint for an AI Brand Ambassador. You must base everything strictly on the Brand File.

Critical rules
1. Zero questions. If the Brand File is vague, make the best trust building decision and include it as a short note in section 12.
2. Be decisive and specific. You must output ONE final character, not ranges.
   a. Age must be an exact number, not a range.
   b. Location must be a specific real city and state, not a generic region.
   c. Name must be a full name, first and last.
3. Consistency is the priority. You must define locked features that never change.
4. Recognition marker system. Define:
   a. One hard locked recognition marker that must appear in all future images.
   b. One optional recognition marker family with 3 options that can vary during casting only.
5. Recognition marker constraints:
   a. Use eyeglasses as the hard locked recognition marker.
   b. Use a flattering beauty mark as part of the optional marker family.
   c. Do not use scars, bruises, or anything that feels like an injury or discomfort cue.
6. Names rule:
   a. Output ONE final full name (first and last) for the character inside section 2.
   b. Also provide 5 additional full name options for backup selection, written inside section 2 under a line called: "Backup full name options (5):"
   c. All names must fit the Brand File audience and the character's city and vibe. Use common, believable names for the market.
7. Do not write production image prompts. Do not write lighting setups or camera moves for image generation. This is a blueprint plus a minimal prototype string only.
8. Default rule. The character appears with the product unless the user explicitly requests otherwise in later prompts.
9. Product info integrity. You must not invent product claims or specs beyond what is in the Brand File.

Prompt 3: The Generator (Casting Auditions)

When to use: After Prompt 2, once you have a completed Character Blueprint.

How to use: Copy the full Character Blueprint ‚Üí Paste the prompt below ‚Üí Paste Blueprint after CHARACTER BLUEPRINT STARTS HERE ‚Üí Generate 15 square portraits in Nano Banana ‚Üí Pick top 3 ‚Üí Pick 1 final

Important: Square portraits. Short prompts. No product. These are casting candidates, not final locked identity.

Act as an expert AI portrait prompt engineer for Nano Banana.

I will paste a Character Blueprint below. Your job is to create 15 copy paste ready prompts for generating 15 different candidate people for the AI Brand Ambassador.

Core objective
Generate 15 distinct faces to act as casting options for the same role. They must all fit the Character Blueprint.

Critical rules
1. Use only the Character Blueprint as your source. Do not ask questions.
2. Output only 15 prompts plus a selection checklist.
3. Do not include product, logos, text on screen, or brand claims.
4. Portrait only. Square framing. Close up or medium close up. Face must be clearly visible.
5. Photorealistic real person. Natural skin texture, visible pores, minor imperfections. No plastic smoothing. No stylized look.
6. Natural looking light. Avoid dramatic cinematic lighting.
7. Eyeglasses rule for casting
   Eyeglasses must be clearly visible in every prompt.
   You may vary eyeglasses style during casting, but only within the optional eyeglasses style options defined in the Character Blueprint.
   Do not introduce any eyeglasses style outside the Blueprint options.
8. Face variation rule
   Each prompt must produce a clearly different face. Use a specific face archetype per prompt, and make structural changes in facial shape, eye shape, nose shape, cheekbones, and jawline.
9. Beauty mark rule for casting
   If a beauty mark is used, it must always be placed above the eyebrow so it is clearly visible.
   Vary only within the beauty mark options defined in the Blueprint, but keep the placement above the eyebrow in all cases.
10. Optional marker family rule
   You may vary only within the optional marker family options defined in the Blueprint, to explore taste. Do not introduce new markers outside that family.
11. Grouping rule for variation
   If the Blueprint includes 3 eyeglasses style options, split the 15 prompts into 3 groups of 5, using one eyeglasses style option per group.
   If the Blueprint includes multiple beauty mark placement options, use them across the 15 prompts, but always above the eyebrow.
12. Keep prompts short. Max 4 sentences.
13. Avoid over specifying. Use only enough detail to guide realism.

Output format
Return only the following sections.

SECTION A. 15 NANO BANANA PROMPTS
Write 15 prompts. Number them 1 to 15. Each prompt must be ready to paste.

Each prompt must include
a. Demographics from the blueprint
b. Hair description from the blueprint
c. Eyeglasses, clearly visible
d. One specific face archetype line plus one structural variation line
e. One optional marker family choice if relevant, including beauty mark above eyebrow only
f. Realism constraints
g. Optional. A simple environment hint, but keep it minimal

Face archetype menu
Use one primary archetype per prompt, and reuse none more than 3 times across the 15.
Pick from:
1 round face, soft jawline
2 oval face, balanced proportions
3 square face, strong jawline
4 heart shaped face, wider forehead, tapered chin
5 diamond face, strong cheekbones, narrower forehead and chin
6 long face, elongated proportions
7 short face, compact proportions
8 angular face, sharp cheekbones and jaw angles
9 soft face, gentle contours, less angular
10 wide face, broader cheek to cheek
11 narrow face, slimmer cheek to cheek
12 prominent cheekbones, defined midface
13 softer midface, less pronounced cheekbones
14 deeper set eyes look, strong brow structure
15 large bright eyes look, softer brow structure

Add these realism tags at the end of every prompt
Raw photo, 35mm look, shallow depth of field, natural skin texture, visible pores, minor imperfections, soft film grain, natural daylight

Write a short checklist of what to look for when choosing the final character. Limit to 8 bullet points.
